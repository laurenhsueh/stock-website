Price vs Sentiment Correlation Chart  ↓

Price vs Sentiment Correlation Chart (Reddit)

Correlation between sentiment and price movements from r/wallstreetbets.

Price vs Sentiment Correlation Chart (Musk)

Correlation between sentiment and price movements from Musk's tweets.

Insights:
1. No Consistent Lead-Lag Relationship
   → Sometimes sentiment increases before a price rise (e.g., late May), suggesting possible predictive power.
   → But in many other cases, price and sentiment diverge, or sentiment is reactive.

2. High Volatility in Sentiment
   → Sentiment spikes up and down frequently, even when price is flat. Reddit sentiment especially shows noisy behavior.

3. General Trend Decoupling
   → From April to August, sentiment remains high while price moves sideways — showing sentiment doesn’t always track price.

Trading Volume vs Reddit Comments Chart (Reddit)

Relationship between trading volume and Reddit comment activity.

Trading Volume vs Musk Tweet Activity Chart (Musk)

Relationship between trading volume and Musk Twitter activity.

(The Musk chart displays trading volume and Musk tweet activity only for the 40 days with the most extreme average sentiment (20 highest and 20 lowest). The x-axis does not represent a continuous timeline, but rather highlights days with unusually high or low sentiment in Musk's tweets.)

Insights:
Spike Alignment
   → Reddit comment spikes often coincide with trading volume surges (e.g., late Jan, early March).
   → Musk tweet bursts also sometimes precede or align with volume changes, though less consistently.

Asymmetry in Influence
   → Not all volume spikes follow social activity. But major Reddit spikes almost always come with volume.

No Obvious Lag
   → Social and market activity tend to peak at the same time.
