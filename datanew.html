But in many other cases, price and sentiment diverge, or sentiment is reactive (i.e., it shifts after price moves). 2. High Volatility in Sentiment Sentiment spikes up and down frequently, even when price is relatively flat (e.g., July 2021). This suggests Reddit sentiment is noisy and possibly influenced by non-price-related factors like memes, hype, or unrelated market chatter. 3. General Trend Decoupling From April to August, average sentiment remains mostly elevated, while the stock price moves sideways to slightly down. This decoupling suggests that high average sentiment doesn't always translate into upward price momentum.

Insights & Trends Positive Correlation Trend (Weak but Present): As sentiment increases toward the right (more positive), there’s a slight increase in the concentration of green dots (positive returns). Conversely, more red dots cluster near the left (negative sentiment), especially around and below 0. Most Negative Returns Cluster Near Neutral Sentiment: Many price drops occur even at mild-to-moderate sentiment levels (0.10–0.25). This suggests sentiment might not always provide enough momentum or signal strength on its own to predict up or down moves. Outliers: There's a massive outlier at ~0.18 sentiment and +20% next-day return — likely an unusual spike driven by a major event. These rare extremes skew visual interpretation but highlight that sentiment spikes can occasionally align with major moves.

Volatility in Sentiment Sentiment is highly volatile, with frequent sharp swings day-to-day. This volatility often does not translate into corresponding price shifts, weakening its predictive power. Possible Local Correlations In some time windows (e.g., early March, mid-May), a drop in sentiment seems to precede or accompany price declines, but these are infrequent and not reliably spaced. You’d need statistical lag correlation tests (e.g., cross-correlation functions) to determine whether any meaningful lag exists.

Key Observations Spike Alignment There are multiple points where comment spikes align with volume surges: Most notably in late January, early March, and early August, both trading volume and Reddit comments spike simultaneously. This implies Reddit hype may amplify market participation, likely from retail traders. Asymmetry in Influence Some volume spikes occur without a Reddit comment spike, suggesting other factors (e.g., earnings reports, macro events) also drive trading volume. But every major comment spike seems to coincide with higher volume, which supports the idea that Reddit chatter contributes to increased trading activity, especially on the retail side. Lag Not Obvious There’s no clear lag (e.g., Reddit comments leading volume by a day or two). Peaks tend to happen concurrently.
